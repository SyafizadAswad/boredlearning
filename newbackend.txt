In the context of the IT industry and software engineering, the term "Agile Labo" (Agile Lab) generally refers to one of three different things depending on the context: a software development model, a collaborative DX (Digital Transformation) hub run by major Japanese tech firms, or a specific company/service name.

Here is a breakdown of what "Agile Labo" usually means:

1. A Development Model: "Agile × Lab-style Development"
This is a highly popular approach in modern software engineering, especially for startups, R&D projects, and DX initiatives.

What is Lab-style Development (ラボ型開発)?
Instead of contracting a vendor to deliver a specific, pre-defined product (like a traditional waterfall fixed-price contract), you secure a dedicated team of engineers for a fixed period (e.g., 6 months to a year).

Why it's paired with Agile:
Because the team is explicitly dedicated to you, it pairs perfectly with Agile frameworks like Scrum. Requirements don't need to be set in stone from day one; you can continuously pivot, refine features, and launch MVPs (Minimum Viable Products) through rapid sprints without renegotiating contracts.

2. Collaborative Innovation Hubs by Major Japanese Tech Firms
Several major IT enterprises in Japan use "Agile Lab" as the name for physical or virtual spaces where they collaborate with clients to build software and train them in Agile methodologies.

FUJITSU Agile Lab:
Fujitsu’s initiative and dedicated spaces designed to help client companies experience real Agile development, accelerate internal software engineering capabilities, and co-create new business models.

AXLab (BIPROGY, formerly Nihon Unisys):
Short for Agile Transformation Lab. It is a service where client teams work side-by-side with experts to learn Agile mindsets and practices—such as mob programming and crafting inception decks.

KAG (KDDI Agile Development Center):
Known for setting up specialized agile hubs, recently focusing heavily on combining offshore agile teams with AI-driven development practices for rapid PoC (Proof of Concept) testing.

3. Specific Companies or Service Brands
There are also legal entities and distinct services operating under this exact name in Japan.

Agile Lab Co., Ltd. (株式会社アジル・ラボ):
An established IT company based in Nakano, Tokyo. They specialize in network system consulting, infrastructure design/construction, and web application development.

"Agile Lab." by RIT Inc.:
A subscription-based, monthly-fee Agile development service provided by the DX consulting firm RIT. It targets businesses looking to build and scale new digital products rapidly.
